# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders or Sponsors are executive leaders or business representatives who provide strategic direction, approve project scope and resources, and make high-level decisions affecting the project's viability and priority. They serve as advocates and decision-makers for the initiative.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project charter and scope
- Allocate resources and budget
- Provide business context and strategic alignment
- Serve as escalation point for major decisions
- Review and approve major milestone completions and release gates
- Remove organizational blockers and competing priorities

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers business value and ROI
- Maintain alignment with organizational strategy
- Minimize scope creep and resource conflicts
- Enable team success through strategic support and sponsorship

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and milestone reviews
- Approval of project charters and phase gates
- Escalation handling and executive-level decision sign-offs
- Ad-hoc communication during significant risks, changes, or decisions

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Approve business cases and strategic priorities
- **Project Managers**: Monitor schedule and resource allocation
- **Developers**: May provide technical feasibility input on strategic decisions
- **Others**: Receive status updates and provide approvals

---

## QA Lead / Test Manager

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and execute quality assurance strategy, establish testing practices, and ensure products meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release. They are accountable for quality assurance throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define QA strategy, test plans, and testing approach
- Create and maintain acceptance criteria validation workflows
- Manage test cases, test coverage, and test automation strategy
- Conduct manual testing and coordinate automation testing efforts
- Report on quality metrics, defect status, and test coverage
- Identify and escalate quality risks and blockers
- Coordinate with developers on bug fixes and regression testing
- Validate release readiness through smoke testing

### Goals
- Ensure delivered features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Maintain quality standards and reduce defects in production
- Provide confidence in release readiness
- Improve quality through continuous testing improvements and metrics

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and QA approach discussions
- Test status reports in weekly delivery syncs
- Defect logs and quality metrics dashboards
- Release readiness assessments and smoke test results
- Quality escalations to Project Manager when needed

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, bug fixes, and regression testing strategies
- **Product Managers**: Refine and validate acceptance criteria with developers
- **Project Managers**: Report quality status, blockers, and release readiness
- **Stakeholders**: Provide quality assurance confidence for release approvals
- **Technical Lead**: Coordinate on automation architecture and test infrastructure

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architectural guidance, review technical decisions, manage technical dependencies, and ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with technical standards. They serve as the technical authority and mentor on the project.

### Responsibilities
- Review and approve technical designs and architecture decisions
- Identify and manage technical dependencies and cross-team impacts
- Mentor developers on technical best practices and design patterns
- Advise on technology choices and evaluate technical trade-offs
- Participate in design reviews and code reviews for complex components
- Ensure alignment with technical standards, security practices, and performance requirements
- Escalate technical blockers, risks, and complex issues
- Provide technical feasibility input on product roadmap decisions

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable, and scalable solutions
- Reduce technical debt and architectural risk
- Enable team learning and skill development through mentoring
- Ensure performance, security, and reliability standards are met
- Facilitate knowledge sharing and technical decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Technical design review meetings and architecture discussions
- Code review comments and technical guidance in PRs
- Architecture and dependency documentation
- Technical risk escalations and feasibility assessments
- Knowledge-sharing sessions and technical mentoring

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Partner on technical decisions, design patterns, and mentoring; approve architecture
- **Product Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility, constraints, and implementation trade-offs
- **Project Managers**: Report technical risks, blockers, and timeline impacts
- **Stakeholders**: Provide input on architectural decisions affecting roadmap or major initiatives
- **QA Lead**: Coordinate on test automation architecture and quality assurance strategy

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Coordinator

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters or Delivery Coordinators facilitate team ceremonies, remove impediments, maintain team velocity, and coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement. They are servant-leaders focused on team enablement and process excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove team blockers and impediments
- Track sprint velocity, burndown metrics, and team capacity
- Coach team on agile practices, ceremonies, and continuous improvement
- Manage and maintain project board and workflow status
- Communicate blockers to Project Manager for cross-team escalation
- Foster psychological safety, team collaboration, and healthy team dynamics
- Facilitate retrospectives and drive implementation of action items

### Goals
- Maintain consistent team velocity and predictability
- Minimize ceremony overhead while maximizing value and engagement
- Empower team to self-organize, solve problems, and improve continuously
- Support continuous improvement through retrospectives and action tracking
- Create a high-performing, psychologically safe team environment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and blocker tracking
- Sprint planning and estimation sessions
- Sprint review and demo facilitation
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking
- Blocker escalation to Project Manager or Team Lead
- Velocity and metric reporting to Project Manager

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Facilitate ceremonies, remove impediments, track team health and velocity
- **Product Managers**: Coordinate sprint priorities, backlog readiness, and acceptance criteria clarity
- **Project Managers**: Escalate blockers, report velocity, flag schedule or resource risks
- **Stakeholders**: Provide team metrics and velocity updates in milestone reviews
- **Technical Lead**: Coordinate on technical priorities and blockers
